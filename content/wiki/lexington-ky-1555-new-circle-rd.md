***Lexington, KY (1555 New Circle Rd) #156*** originally opened up on December 1, 1983{{< cite 1 >}}, as a Showbiz Pizza Place {{< cite 1 >}} and would close on August 3, 2025, as a Chuck E. Cheese. {{< cite 2 >}}

## History
### Pre-Opening
On May 25, 1983 the Lexington Herald-Leader announced that Lexington was planned to get a ShowBiz Pizza Place in October 1983. 'There will be a game room with more than 70 video games, a Ferris wheel and merry-g-round, computerized robots singing pop tunes from the '50s and '60s, and a "quiet" room with a giant TV screen{{< cite 3 >}}.
August 18, 1983 a notice of intention was posted on part of McBiz Corporation "to apply for a retail beer license, resturant, wine dealer license"{{< cite 4 >}} By August 28, 1983 the McBiz Corporation was "seeking experienced Electronics Technicians" {{< cite 5 >}}

### Opening
Lexington would open on December 1, 1983. The location would be the second Showbiz Pizza Place to open in the state of Kentucky. The store would also feature a bawl crawl as an attraction.{{< cite 6 >}}
On March 5, 1984 Hamill & McKinny Architects - Engineers - Interdesigners would mention working on the Lexington Showbiz Pizza Place.{{< cite 7 >}}

### 1980's
On Feburary 18, 1984 ShowBiz Pizza Place would take assist in selling cookies for the Girl Scouts. "Mary Bacallo, ShowBiz Pizza Place community relations director, said troops could come to the business to sell cookies or that "Billy Bob," the resturant's bear mascot, "will go out with the girls door-to-door"{{< cite 8 >}} Between April 1st through April 8th 1984 The Lexington ShowBiz Pizza Place would hold a color contest to see who could best color "Pot Belly Bunny, Renaldo and Bottle Bunny" the contest would require the colored photos to be brought to Stewart's children's deptartment where you would win a Billy Bob Buck. The winner would receieve a free party for six, one large pizza, a pitcher of soft drinks, $5 in game tokens and a plush bunny. Judging would be based upon "neatness, originality and overall appearance. Entrants will be placed in 2 age groups -- 3-6 and 7-11, with one winner in each group", The winners would be announced on April 14, where Billy Bob would be present from 2 - 4 p.m.{{< cite 9 >}} On May 25, 1984 Billy Bob would be at the Polar Bear Swim at Tales Creek Pool.{{< cite 9 >}} On June 3, 1984 Billy Bob would make an appearance at "The Festival of the Bluegrass" {{< cite 10 >}} On October 30th and October 31st at 7:30p.m. a "Safe Halloween Celebration" would take place at the ShowBiz Pizza Place. Every child in costume would get a treat and prize, Costume Contest, and a Haunted House was constructed exclusively being stated as "one entire room at ShowBiz decorated ghoulishly, free admission!" {{< cite 11 >}} Lexington's ShowBiz would participate in the New Year's Countdown promotion happening on New Year's Eve from 11 a.m. - 1 a.m. and New Year's Day from 4 p.m. - closing {{< cite 12 >}} 

On February 15th through the 17th, 1985 ShowBiz Pizza Place would host Charlie Myrick "One of the World's Greatest Magicians!" {{< cite 13 >}} On May 22nd through the 24th, 1985 ShowBiz Pizza Place would present Clifford Guest "A Ventriloquist With A Difference" at 5 p.m., 6 p.m. & 7 p.m. {{< cite 14 >}}
On June 28, 1985 Billy Bob's Birthday Celebration would take place featuring all day double tokens, free silce of birthday cake, free autographed pictures of Billy Bob, and Door Prize Drawings at 7:00 p.m. with potential winnings being "1 Birthday Party for 6, 2 "Family Night Out" packages, and 1 Video Game" with the M.C. being WVLK Disc Jockey Will from 11 a.m to 1 p.m. {{< cite 14 >}} On December 1, 1985 from 5 to 7pm Santa's Arrival Party would take place! On December 1st through December 22nd 5 p.m. to 7 p.m. Lexington's ShowBiz allowed guests to "have dinner with Santa and Billy Bob!". {{< cite 15 >}} Lexington's ShowBiz would participate in the New Year's Countdown promotion happening on New Year's Eve from 11 a.m. - 1 a.m. and New Year's Day from 4 p.m. - 10 p.m. {{< cite 16 >}} 

All day on October 30th and October 31st, 1986 Lexington's ShowBiz would have Safe Halloween Parties! Featuring the same events as last year with the addition that Fan Club Members get 2 free tokens. {{< cite 17 >}} 

During Feburary 1987 Free Sugarless Drinks, Free Popcorn and Pictures with the tooth fairy (for $2.00) would take place to celebrate Children's Dental Health Month. The event would return in 1988 although without the tooth fairy. {{< cite 19 >}}
All day on October 30th and October 31st, 1987 Lexington's ShowBiz would have Safe Halloween Parties! "Every child in costume receives a free treat, Parade of Costumes, Exciting games and activites! FREE Face Painting!". {{< cite 20 >}}

During Feburary 1988 Free Sugarless Drinks and Free Popcorn would be avalible. {{< cite 18 >}} In July 1988 Mrs. Alexander, executive director of the Kentucky Commission on Women held a party which featured Billy Bob from ShowBiz Pizza Place. {{< cite 21 >}}

Between 1988 and 1990 Lexington would recieve the {{< wiki-link "SPT 1980's Remodel Program" >}} and would install the {{< wiki-link "ShowBiz Pizza Campground" >}} on its {{< wiki-link "Rock-afire Explosion" >}}
