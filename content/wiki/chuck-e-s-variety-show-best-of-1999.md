**Chuck E.'s Variety Show** was a showtape initially released in January 2000, which played normally through March 2000. This show was considered generic and could be used as a backup show until 2003.
This show is primarily composed of material from 1997 to 1999, with a few intermissions from 1994 as well.

## Trivia:

- This show replaces {{< wiki-link "Best of CEC TV 1993" >}} as the emergency backup show for Existing Stages.
- 'A Day At The Movies' is reused from the {{< wiki-link "Patriotic 1994 Show" >}}. 'Laugh Tracks', 'Chuck E.'s Big Bowling Date' and 'The Virtuoso' are reused from {{< wiki-link "September '94" >}}. 'Will Frye The Nutrition Guy', 'The Announcer', 'Pondwatch', 'Name That Cheese', and Larry The Technician - 'Video Feed' are reused from {{< wiki-link "Studio C Premiere" >}}. 'Introducing Larry The Technician' and 'I Like This Game' are reused from {{< wiki-link "September 1998" >}}. 'Disco Chuck', 'Safety Pin', 'Rockin' Robin', and 'Tokens To Ride' are reused from {{< wiki-link "January 1999" >}}. 'Chuck E.'s Dance Party', 'Dancin' and Singin'', 'Singing Cowboy', 'Ratings',   'Hip Mouse', 'Pizza Serenade', Super Jasper - 'Cadilac Ranch', 'Chuck E. Town', Super Jasper - 'Staring Contest', 'Let's Dance', and 'Memories Are Forever' are reused from {{< wiki-link "April 1999" >}}. 'Drawing Chuck E.', 'Cheese Pizza Is Pure Delight', Boat of Love', 'We Sail The Ocean Blue', 'We All Love Music', 'Clowns Making A Sandwich', 'We Are The Same', and 'Baking With Betty' are reused from {{< wiki-link "August 1999" >}}.
- This is the first show to feature the 'Baking with Betty' and 'Clowns Making a Sandwich' intermissions on Existing Stages. Previously, they were only on the Studio C version of {{< wiki-link "August 1999" >}}.
- The Studio C puppet backgrounds are reused from {{< wiki-link "August 1999" >}}. All puppet footage was rerecorded for this show.
- The bluescreen backgrounds for Studio C are reused from their original shows.
- Segment 11 is split into two segments on Studio C. 
- 'Cheese Pizza Is Pure Delight' and 'Hip Mouse' are both sung by Chuck E. on Studio C, just like in their original shows. Chuck E.'s vocals in 'Pizza Serenade', which were previously exclusive to Studio C, are featured in all versions of this show.
