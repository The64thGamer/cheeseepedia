The ***Jingle Bell Jamboree*** (Stylized as JINGLEBELL JAMBOREE) was an Animatronic Show at Santa's Village in Jefferson, New Hampshire from 1977 until it's Removal in 2006. The Show was originally built by Creative Engineering as it's first full show, but was later changed to have Advanced Animations Animatronics from 2010 to 2016.

## History

### Production

The Show originally began production in 1977 as 'The Santa Claus Revue'{{< cite 1 >}} by Creative Engineering. The Concept Art showcased Stage Right with Rudolph, Center Stage with the 3 Elves, Stage Left with Santa, and Elfis and Mrs Claus on the Side Walls. The Show was created for Santa's Village unlike previous CEI Animatronics & Attractions.

### Creative Engineering

Jingle Bell Jamboree was installed at an unknown time in 1977 in Santa's Village's Jingle Bell Theater{{< cite 2 >}}. The Show differed from Concept Art, scrapping Mrs Claus and having Elfis be in the Center of Center Stage after Rudolph's Segment using a Trap Door. The Show also installed 3 small Animal Animatronics. The Show was originally installed with Three 8-Track Reels, but CEI later took a private flight to Santa's Village in 1979 to install a new CEI 4-Track Reel Showtape as a sign of appreciation{{< cite 2 >}}.
The Show eventually began to break down, and began removal in 2006{{< cite 3 >}}.

### Advanced Animations

Santa's Village would get Advanced Animation's to redo the Show alongside the Outside Rudolph Head which was also originally developed by Creative Engineering, Creating Concept art and Building the Animatronics between 2009-2010. In 2009, The Show was retrofitted into a Half Animatronic Half Live show called the 'Jingle Bell Talent Show'{{< cite 4 >}}. The Talent Show only Used Rudolph and Elfin John's animatronic retrofitted into an unknown Elf character.

The Show was finished and installed in 2010, using the original showtape. The Show removed the 3 Smaller Animals, and later removed the Red backdrop on the Top Art. The Show continued to run until 2016, in which it was removed.

## Design

### Overall Stage


The Overall stage featured a wooden base with curved panels and two metal grates on each end of center stage. The stage also featured red curtains and garlands that were placed around the edges of the stage, the stage floor, and on the valence. Two JBL 4311B Speakers were placed between the stages with two large candy canes sitting on top. Above the stage was a solid valence that featured red fabric and a fluffy white trim. On the side stages, there were elf faces that represented the Comedy and Tragedy masks often found within Theatre. Stage Right - Tragedy, Stage Left - Comedy. Surrounding the elf faces was a wreath with two candy canes framing it on both sides. Center stage featured a gradient from red to black, with the black acting as the night sky, decorated with rhinestones that acted as the stars, with some forming the North Star in the middle of the valence. On this was Santa riding his Sleigh with a fabric bag of presents, being pulled by 5 Reindeer with two elves holding onto a rope hanging off the back of the Sleigh. Attached to this was fabric valence pieces that would swoop down in circles.

### Stage Right

Stage Right contained Rudolph on with a Black Backdrop and red carpet. The black backdrop only went halfway to the back wall to allow for a backstage where the controller and any other technical things could be stored.

### Center Stage

Center Stage contained Multiple Animatronics. From left to right the Stage had a sandy/gray Dog who wagged its tail, Elfen John on piano (based off of Elton John), a white Rabbit atop of the piano who tapped its foot, Satchmo on the trumpet, Maynard on Bass, and a black/dark gray Cat who tilted its head.
The Stage featured a brown carpet, a Christmas tree behind Maynard, a red and white striped stocking on the wall behind Elfen, a fireplace in the middle with a custom Creative Engineering made painting depicting a cabin in the snowy mountains hung above. On the fireplace were stockings decorated with Rudolph, Drummer, Snowman, and Santa Illustrations. Around the stage were other Christmas Items found inside Stockings, on the fireplace, and even on the Piano, with some examples being Christmas Cards, Angels, Snowmen, and even little elves.
In the middle of the stage was a hole covered by a green carpet. This carpet would pull back, allowing for Elfis (based off of Elvis Presley) to raise up from a platform during the show.

### Stage Left

Stage Left was the same as Stage Right but instead, contained Santa sitting on a rocking chair.

## Design History

Over the years, the stage design would change with many of the loose items being shifted around, with some disappearing and new ones appearing. 
The first big change came in 1991 where center stage was given an upgrade. This upgrade featured a new wooden skirting on the back wall and two large fake windows on either side. This change also removed the large candy canes on either side of Center Stage, the tree behind Maynard, the stocking behind Elfen, and the loose Christmas items from around the stage. The cat was moved to on the Piano next to the Bunny.
The next change came sometime from 1992 to 2006. With this change, Elfis no longer raised from a platform and instead, was hidden behind a very large Christmas tree, skating around it to appear instead of rising from the floor. Some changes made to this was the change of the carpet to this patterned Green Carpet that folded over the edges of the stage. The hole used for the platform now housed the mechanism that turned Elfis around the tree. Elfen John's piano was changed, reusing the keys (and mechanism) from the original and placing it in a new shorter white piano, changing Elfen's orientation to face the audience instead of to the side. As there was now a large tree in the center of the stage, Satchmo and Maynard were also repositioned so that they could be seen. The dog was moved to Santa's stage and the Bunny to Rudy's stage. The show would remain this way until 2006 when it was removed.

### Jingle Bell Talent Show

After the CEI show was removed, it was sent to Advanced Animations to be used as reference for creating the replacement show. During the next few years as the new show was being made, Santa's Village needed a new show to play during their open season and so, very last minute, the Jingle Bell Talent Show was developed featuring two live performing actors who would sing and dance, featuring two of the new animatronics who were completed. Rudy acting as the announcer on Stage Right, and a female version of Elfen John on Stage Left. Center Stage was given a golden streamer backdrop with two large colored lights on either side and a large sign saying "North Pole Talent Show LIVE".

### Advanced Animations Show

When the show was switched to the Advanced Animations show, the stage (and theatre) were redone. The stage base kept the wooden panels and 2 metal grates but was now decorated with a thick garland with 3 Nut Crackers placed in front. This would later change to a white garland with the nut crackers being removed. The stage kept the red curtains but switched the valence piece to a blue fabric, keeping the custom decorations, adding some ornaments and sparkly trees, but removing the Candy Canes, Wreaths, and Fabric bits that swooped down.

#### Side Stages

The side stages changed the black backdrop to be on only one side, adding a blue backdrop to the other, using the same fabric as the valence. The stages also now featured multi colored presents and other Christmas decorations such as Nut Crackers.

#### Center Stage

Center stage reused the back wall with the fake windows and fireplace but replaced the painting with a wreath. The painting now currently resides in the Owner's office, hung on the wall. The stage also re-add the platform for Elfis to rise from, allowing the other characters to be placed back in their original positions. Satchmo now stood on a large green present with other presents surrounding it. This version lacked animals.

## Animatronics (from left to right)

#### Rudy (Rudolph)

Rudy/Rudolph is a reindeer featuring a latex mask covered in a dark brown fur with white details on the snout. He has a red nose and Antlers that were replaced sometime between 1992 to 2006. He has blue eyes with no pupils. He wears a Tuxedo with no pants and holds a microphone that rests on a mic stand that swings as he moves.

#### Props (Dog, Bunny, Cat)

The props are made of Fiberglass that is painted and covered in fur - They only have 1 movement each (Tail Wag, Foot Tap, Head Tilt)

#### Elfen John (Parody of Elton John) 

Elfen is an elf that sits at a piano, wearing a green shirt and tights with a red vest and shorts with fluffy white trims. He has large green glasses with rhinestones decorating the frames as well as a santa hat atop of his head. He has blonde curly hair and a large nose that moves up and down.

#### Satchmo (Parody of Louis Armstrong)

Satchmo an elf positioned in front of the Fireplace, wearing a green shirt and tights with a red vest and shorts with fluffy white trims. He has brown straight hair with a little strand poking out from his Santa hat. His eyes pop in and out of his face as he blows his trumpet.

#### Maynard

Maynard is an elf positioned in front of the Christmas Tree on the right side of the stage. He wears a green shirt and tights with a red vest and shorts with fluffy white trims. He wears black sunglasses with blondish hair poking out from under his Santa Hat. he plays the Cello which can rock backwards and forwards as he plays, and he shares a mask with Elfis.

#### Elfis (Parody of Elvis Presley)

Elfis is an elf positioned in the middle of center stage on a platform that rises from the ground. He is dressed in light denim pants, a white shirt and a leather jacket with slicked back black hair. He holds a microphone in his right hand and holds Crotal Bells (Jingle Bells) in his left that jingle as he performs.

#### Santa Claus

Santa is in his classic red Santa Suit with white fluffy trims, a black belt, and big black boots. He has his white fluffy beard as well as his large Santa Hat. He also wears red mittens and sits on a wooden rocking chair that rocks as he performs.
