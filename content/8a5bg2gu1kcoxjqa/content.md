***Cyberamics Betamax*** is an obscure predecessor to the [C&R SVHS (Pre-93)] format that was tested at the [Arlington, TX (2216 S Fielder Rd)] from January 15, 1987, until some time in 1988.
The format was used to synchronize video with showtapes and to combine the main show with the previously separate [Showbiz Pizza Programs] and [Family Vision] tapes. [4]

## History

Cybervision was first demonstrated to members of the SPT Entertainment Committee at the programming studio in the Corporate Office during a meeting on September 18, 1986. [3]

On January 15, 1987, the [Arlington, TX (2216 S Fielder Rd)] store began testing the first iteration of Cybervision using Betamax tapes. [4] This would be done for every tape of that year before Cybervision would make its way to the rest of the stores in early 1988. These tapes would use minimum system features in order to ensure essential features could work feasibly, utilizing only the main show, background music, a basic countdown, and Family Vision. [2] The switch from Reel to Cybervision resulted in certain features having to be discontinued, including Interactive Animation and non-birthday Special skits included on the tape. [2]

A quote from David Philipson many years ago also discusses the system, though he notes *two* stores had the old system and were instead in Austin, TX. It is unclear if these were two extra stores, or if he meant to say Arlington, or if one was in Arlington and one in Austin. There being two stores also leads to the idea that the other store was indeed a ShowBiz store, as shown by the RAE Betamax tape. [1] Another option is that the second store is [White Settlement, TX (7750 Scott St)], which was the main store filmed for Cybervision/Cyberstar and training videos.

The [Cyberstar Diagnostic Tape] for the Rock-afire was notably produced in 1988 on Betamax. It has not been confirmed if this tape was used like previous [CEI Betamax] tapes on an external monitor, or if it was produced for the early tests of Cyberstar. Only one copy of the tape has ever been found.

Given the earliest Cybervision showtape was Tune Machine / Country, and that the Betamax stores were in the test market area, this is likely when the prototype format was phased out for Cybervision, but it still could have taken place later in the year at either store.

### Reused & Updated Footage

Eventually, Cybervision would swap its footage style from filming at [White Settlement, TX (7750 Scott St)], and instead filmed at the CEC Corporate offices with a bluescreen. The last showtape to film at White Settlement was [CEC Dance Showtape] in September of 1988, and the first at corporate was [CEC Fractured Classics] in January of 1989. The segments from 1987's [CEC Home for the Holidays] would be used again in 1988, though no video would be shown for them. [Chuck E. Cheese Shows (CEC Best of 1988)] would reuse segments from Country, Faves, and Tune Machine. The Tune Machine video is of White Settlement, but the videos for Faves and Country were newly recorded at Corporate. The 1987 Holiday segments would also have new video recorded at Corporate for [CEC Christmas '89 / Wave Bday], and three songs from [CEC TV Westerns] would have new footage recorded for [CEC TV Classics].
