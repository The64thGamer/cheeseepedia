Jackson, TN (2021 North Highland Ave) #667 originally opened up on July 30, 2003 as a Chuck E. Cheese’s[1] and would close on November 23, 2025, as a Chuck E. Cheese's.[2]

## History

### 2000's

The Jackson, TN, Chuck E. Cheese's opened July 29, 2003, inside of Old Hickory Mall in the former Blockbuster space[3]. The store featured an [Studio C Cappa] (Mirror), New Games, Decor from the [SPT 1990's Remodel Program]. It would have a grand opening celebration featuring a Chamber of Commerce ribbon cutting  at 9a.m.[1] 

### 2010's

Between 2008 and 2012 Jackson would replace its Cool Chuck attire with the full Avenger Chuck attire. 
In 2012, Jackson would recieve the [CEC 2000's Remodel Program] during the remodel the Studio C Cappa would be relocated across the store. During the relocation the blue screen camera would be removed. In mid 2016 to early 2017 the store recieved full rockstar attire for the [Chuck E. Cheese 16m (Animatronic)]. In 2017 [Skytubes] In late 2019 the Rockstar shirt would be replaced with the Avenger shirt. [4]

### 2020's

March 2020 would mark the store's temporary closing due to COVID-19. As early as July 2020, the store would re-open.[5].
Between 2021 and 2022 the bluescreen would be painted black. Between 2023 and 2024 one of the TVs and one bar of lights above the former bluescreen would be removed.[4] The store would also be in the [Low Volume Store Initiative] at some time with just the reduced hours portion.[5]

In January 2025 the Trampoline Zone, Chuck E. Cheese and Mr. Munch Trampoline Zone posters would be installed[6]. In mid-late 2025, it would be reported that CEC Entertainment had sent employees to the location to take an inventory, alongside pictures of every single item in the store.[7]

On November 9, 2025 it would be announced on Facebook via 'What’s Happening In Jackson TN?' that the Jackson, TN Chuck E. Cheese's would be closing on November 23, 2025 it was stated '...the only way you can book a party is by going inside the store to speak with management since they can no longer book online or by phone'[2] On the final day, before the store had even closed, artwork was being removed from the walls. Managers had also apparently gave items to fans visiting. It is unclear what items were given away, and what were sent into storage. The location would finally close on November 23, 2025 after 22 years of operation, this was the last location to have the V1 of [Studio C Cappa].[7]
